### About SCCP

SCCP is a new venue dedicated to the formal verification, runtime checking, and principled analysis of consistency models in distributed systems, databases, and concurrent programs.

As distributed systems proliferate across cloud infrastructure, edge networks, and mobile platforms, ensuring that data remains consistent — or reasoning precisely about when and how it may diverge — has become a central challenge. SCCP brings together researchers and practitioners from distributed systems, programming languages, formal methods, and databases to advance the state of the art in consistency checking.

The workshop focuses on *checking*: how can we verify, test, monitor, or certify that a system satisfies a given consistency model?

### Topics of Interest

Topics of interest include (but are not limited to):

* Formal verification of consistency models (linearizability, serializability, causal consistency, snapshot isolation, etc.)
* Runtime monitoring and testing for consistency violations
* Automated checking tools and frameworks
* Consistency in distributed databases and key-value stores
* Weak memory models and hardware consistency checking
* Consistency in replicated data structures (CRDTs)
* Checking consistency in geo-distributed and edge systems
* Trade-offs between consistency, availability, and performance
* Consistency properties of transactional systems
* Case studies and experience reports from production systems
* Synthesis of correct-by-construction consistent systems
